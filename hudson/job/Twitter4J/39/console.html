  <h3 class="entry-title"><a href="/forums/39781/entries/29147">Replies tab now includes mentions!</a></h3>
  <p class="sub"> 
    Submitted Mar 26 in <strong>Twitter Announcements and Features!</strong>
  </p>
  <div class="user_formatted" style="margin-top:26px;"><p><h1>Changes to your replies tab</h1>
<p>Twitter has made some changes to the way replies work.  Before, only replies (tweets beginning with @username) showed up in the replies tab.  Now, the replies tab instead shows your user name, and both replies <strong>and</strong> mentions (tweets with @username anywhere in the tweet) appear there.</p>
<p><img src="/attachments/422172" border="0" alt="Twitter____crystal.jpg" /></p>
<h1>Replies and Mentions</h1>
<p>Twitter has always placed all tweets starting with your user name in your replies tab. 
For example, the tweet below is a reply written by @amritsingh,  to @elizaberkowitz.  She will see this message in her replies tab because it begins with the @ sign + her user name.</p>
<p><img src="/attachments/404492" border="0" alt="Twitter___amrit_singh___elizaberkowitz_That_this_...-1.jpg" /></p>
<p>Twitter has always defined an update that begins with @username as a reply; when @username occurs elsewhere in an update, we refer to it as a 'mention.'</p>
<h1><br /></h1>
<h1>Mentions are now included in your replies tab</h1>
<p>A tweet that contains @username in the body of the update is considered a mention.  In the update below, @pitchforkmedia appears in the middle of the tweet. This is considered a mention. </p>
<p><img src="/attachments/404494" border="0" alt="Twitter___amrit_singh__This_isn_t_taco_related__b_...-1.jpg" /></p>
<p>We noticed people frequently searching for their user name (@username) to find the tweets that mention their user name. We decided to save some folks some trouble, and include mentions under the replies tab.</p>
<p> </p>
<h1>Does this affect my replies settings?<br /></h1>
<p>Nope! The replies setting only affects what you see in your home page.  There is no setting for the replies tab.  .</p>
<p> </p>
<h1>Do I have to change anything? What should I expect?</h1>
<p>This is a system wide change that is effective immediately, and you
don't have to change a thing.  Expect more updates: now all tweets in reply to you as well as all tweets
mentioning you by user name will appear in the sidebar under your user name.</p>
<p> </p>

  <h3 class="entry-title"><a href="/forums/10713/entries/14362">Why is my account suspended?</a></h3>
  <p class="sub"> 
    Submitted Nov 13, 2008 in <strong>Trouble Shooting</strong>
  </p>
  <div class="user_formatted" style="margin-top:26px;"><p><p><img src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3242/3027633219_29595cb081_m.jpg" border="0" height="178" alt="twitter suspended account owl icon" width="240" /></p>
<p>Twitter suspends account for suspicious activity.  Suspended accounts are researched by our Spam/Abuse or Support team.  One or more of these things may cause an account to be suspended:</p>
<h3>Technical abuse of the Twitter Service</h3>
<ul>
<li>unauthorized scripting of the site<br /></li>
<li>unauthorized serial account creation</li>
<li>unauthorized mass account creation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Spam investigation</h3>
<p>Publishing, posting or sending unsolicited email, junk mail, “spam”, chain letters, promotions, or advertisements for products or services (except where expressly permitted) is against the Terms of Service.  Commercial or promotional use of Twitter is allowed, and we do welcome
feed-based accounts. Many
companies create valuable, opt-in relationships with people on
Twitter.  It's important to us, however, that the Twitter community
receives only the content they'd like to receive.  The following behaviors may cause an account to get flagged for spam investigation and/or suspension:</p>
<ul>
<li>user name squatting</li>
<li>account contains links pointing to phishing sites, malware, or other harmful material</li>
<li>an account is identified as belonging to a group of spam accounts</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>a large number of people block the profile in question<br /> </li>
<li>a large number of people write in with spam complaints for a specific profile</li>
<li>aggressive following (a large number of people are followed in a short amount of time)</li>
<li>extremely imbalanced follower/following ratio<br /></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>updates consist of duplicate or repeating links and/or text</li>
<li>updates consist mainly of links and not personal updates</li>
<li>links in updates disguise the real content of a link given in a misleading or deceptive way.<br /></li>
<li>unauthorized "re-tweets" (poaching and posting other users' updates) passed off as original content* <br /></li>
</ul>
<p>If one or more the items listed above is true for any Twitter
profile, we may suspend the account for abuse investigation and hide
the contents from the public view in order to remove the cause of
complaint. If you think you've been wrongly
suspended, please send an email to <a href="mailto:suspended@twitter.com">suspended@twitter.com</a> with the user
name and reason why the account shouldn't be suspended.</p>
<h3>Terms of Service violations</h3>
<p>As stated in the Terms of Service, Twitter reserves the right to
immediately terminate accounts without further notice if the account is
in violation of the Terms of Service.  If your account is suspended
however, we're most likely researching an issue with the account. If
this is the case, you'll most likely have heard from our Support team.
The following problems fall under Terms of Service violations:</p>
<ul>
<li>Copyright Infringement (To file a complaint, see our copyright infringement procedure <a href="http://help.twitter.com/forums/26257/entries/15795">here</a>.)</li>
<li>Impersonation</li>
<li>Violence and/or specific threats</li>
<li>Unlawful use</li>
<li>Privacy infringement<br /></li>
<li>Trademark Infringement</li>
</ul>
<p>Send all non-copyright  Terms of Service complaints to <a href="mailto:terms@twitter.com">terms@twitter.com</a>.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><em>Contact <a href="mailto:terms@twitter.com">terms@twitter.com</a> for questions or complaints regarding the Terms of Service. Contact <a href="mailto:suspended@twitter.com">suspended@twitter.com</a> if your account has been suspended.</em></p></p></div>

  <h3 class="entry-title"><a href="/forums/10711/entries/14020">The Official Twitter Text Commands</a></h3>
  <p class="sub"> 
    Submitted Nov 06, 2008 in <strong>Getting Started</strong>
  </p>
  <div class="user_formatted" style="margin-top:26px;"><p><div class="ArwC7c ckChnd" id="1fqh">
<h2><span style="font-size: medium;">The Official Twitter Commands </span></h2>
</div>
<div class="ArwC7c ckChnd" id="1fqh">
<h4>Did you know: you can perform certain actions, like following or marking a friend's update as a favorite, by using the designated Twitter commands? 
Use the commands listed below from your phone, the web update box, or your favorite third party application.</h4>
<p> </p>
<h2>Turning Twitter off and on: device notifications </h2>
</div>
<div class="ArwC7c ckChnd" id="1fqh">
<div class="gmail_quote">
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>ON: </strong>turns ALL phone notifications on.</p>
</li>
<li><strong>OFF</strong>: turns ALL phone notifications off. <br /> </li>
<li><strong>STOP, QUIT</strong>: stops all messages to your phone immediately</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>ON username</strong>: turns on notifications for a specific person on your phone. For example, ON alissa.</li>
<li><strong>OFF username</strong>: turns off notifications for a specific person on your phone. For example, OFF blaine.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>FOLLOW username: </strong>this command allows you to start 	receiving notifications for a specific person on your phone. Example: follow jeremy</li>
<li>
<p><strong>LEAVE username: </strong>this command allows you to stop receiving notifications for a specific person on your phone. Example: leave benfu</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>Fun Stuff: friends, favorites, and stats!</h2>
<div class="gmail_quote">There's more to Twitter than OFF and ON! Use the commands below to send
private messages, mark updates as favorites, or even remind someone to
update their Twitter page if you're wondering what they're doing!<br /></div>
<ul>
<li><strong>@username + message<br /> </strong></li>
directs a twitter at another person, and causes your twitter to save in their "replies" tab. <br /> Example: @meangrape I love that song too!<br /><br />
<li><strong>D username + message</strong><br /> </li>
sends a person a private message that goes to their device, and saves in their web archive. <br /> Example: d krissy want to pick a Jamba Juice for me while you're there?<br /><br />
<li> <strong>WHOIS username</strong> <br /> </li>
retrieves the profile information for any public user on Twitter. <br /> Example: whois jack<br /><br />
<li><strong>GET username</strong><br /> </li>
retrieves the latest Twitter update posted by the person. <br /> Example: get goldman<br /><br />
<li><strong>NUDGE username</strong> <br /> </li>
reminds a friend to update by asking what they're doing on your behalf. <br /> Example: nudge biz<br /><br />
<li><strong>FAV username</strong><br /> </li>
marks a person's last twitter as a favorite. (hint: reply to any update
with FAV to mark it as a favorite if you're receiving it in real time) <br /> Example: fav al3x 
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>STATS</strong></li>
this command returns your number of followers, how many people you're following, and which words you're tracking.<br /><br />
<li><strong>INVITE</strong> <strong>phone number<br /> </strong></li>
will send an SMS invite to a friend's mobile phone. <br /> Example: Invite 415 555 1212<br /><br /><br /> 
</ul>
</div>
<div class="gmail_quote">
<h2>Noteworthy Facts</h2>
<ul>
<li> using on/off username from your phone only stops notifications to
the place the command comes from; you'll still collect a person's
updates on the web.</li>
<li> using follow/leave username from your phone is the same as using on/off username</li>
<li> following someone from a phone for the first time will also cause you to follow them on the web</li>
<li> there is no way to stop following a person on the web without
visiting their profile and removing them. The off, leave, stop, and
quit commands will only disable updates for the device(s) from which
they were sent.</li>
<li>you don't have to use ON/OFF username from the phone, you can also set
individual notifcations from a person's profile page, or check your <a href="http://twitter.com/friends">following</a> page and manage all phone notification settings there.</li>
</ul>
